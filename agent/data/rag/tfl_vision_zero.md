# TfL Vision Zero & Healthy Streets (mock excerpt)

Source: Transport for London Vision Zero Action Plan and Healthy Streets Approach (illustrative summary for LondonZero demo).

## Vision Zero principles
- The Safe System approach accepts that people make mistakes; the street must be designed so mistakes do not result in death or serious injury.
- Speed is the fundamental factor. 20 mph limits are the default for most TfL and borough roads in central London.
- Priority is given to the most vulnerable road users: pedestrians first, then cyclists, then public transport.

## Healthy Streets indicators relevant to redesign
- "Easy to cross": frequent, direct, accessible crossings with minimal staggered islands; reduce pedestrian wait times.
- "People feel safe": clear sightlines, removal of guard railing where it traps vulnerable users, good lighting.
- "Not too noisy / clean air": reduced motor traffic dominance and reallocation of carriageway space.

## Pedestrian crossing guidance
- Where pedestrians are involved in 20% or more of collisions, prioritise single-stage signalised crossings, widened refuge islands, and continuous footways across side roads.
- Limited sightlines caused by parked vehicles or street clutter should be addressed with build-outs and parking removal near the crossing.
