# London Cycling Design Standards (LCDS) — Cycle Infrastructure (mock excerpt)

Source: Transport for London, London Cycling Design Standards (illustrative summary for LondonZero demo).

## Separation and protection
- On roads with motor traffic volumes above 5,000 PCU/day or speed limits at or above 30 mph, cyclists should be physically separated from general traffic using protected cycle tracks (kerb-segregated or light-segregated).
- Light segregation (armadillos, wands, planters) is acceptable as an interim measure but kerb separation is preferred at high-casualty junctions.
- Minimum desirable width for a one-way protected cycle track is 2.0 m; absolute minimum 1.5 m.

## Junctions and conflict points
- Left-hook collisions between motor vehicles and cyclists are a priority concern. Provide early-release (head-start) cycle signals or fully separated signal stages where turning conflicts are high.
- Cycle gates and advanced stop lines (ASLs) of at least 5 m depth should be provided where full separation is not feasible.
- Blind spots from large vehicles (HGVs, buses) require setback stop lines and improved direct vision.

## Application to high-casualty City junctions
- Where cyclists are involved in 20% or more of recorded collisions, protected cycle separation on the highest-casualty arms is the recommended primary intervention.
